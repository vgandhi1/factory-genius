# Conveyance drive shaft — Bearing housing thermal rise

## Symptoms

- Pillow block or gearbox input shaft housing runs **hotter than baseline** at the same line speed and load.
- Thermal gradient along the shaft line suggests **drag** at one station only (localized, not whole line).

## Preventive vs breakdown

- **Preventive / condition-based:** Temperature **5–15°C** above baseline, stable trend over shifts → schedule **greasing**, **alignment check**, and **vibration snapshot** on next PM window; keep line running with elevated monitoring.
- **Breakdown / immediate:** **Runaway** temperature, smoke/oil smell, or **>25°C** step change vs baseline → **stop conveyance**, LOTO, inspect coupling and bearing **before** restart.

## Likely causes on rotary trains

1. **Degraded grease** or wrong lubricant interval on the drive or tension shaft.
2. **Misalignment** or **soft foot** loading one bearing.
3. **Over-tensioned belt or chain** adding side load to the shaft.

## Field sequence

1. Confirm speed reference and load match baseline conditions.
2. Compare **drive-end vs idle-end** housing temperatures on the same shaft.
3. If preventive path: log trend, schedule **bearing service** per OEM; if breakdown path: execute **controlled stop** and **thermal soak** before hands-on work.
