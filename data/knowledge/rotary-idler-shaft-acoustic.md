# Rotary idler / roller shaft — Acoustic bearing signatures

## What to listen for (edge acoustic bands)

- **2–6 kHz** broadband rise often tracks **bearing defect** energy on **idler or bend-pulley** shafts.
- **Squeal or chirp** tied to **1× shaft speed** may indicate **misalignment** or **rub** (guard, frame).

## Preventive vs breakdown

- **Preventive:** New noise **without** temperature excursion; RMS trending up slowly → plan **replacement or relube** in next outage; increase inspection frequency.
- **Breakdown:** **Step change** in noise **with** hot housing, visible **wobble**, or **metal in grease** → treat as **failure in progress**; stop and inspect per LOTO.

## Rotary-specific checks

1. Note **belt or chain tension**—over-tension loads idler shafts.
2. Spin rollers by hand **after** LOTO; feel for **gritty** or **notchy** rotation.
3. Replace bearings using OEM **stack-up, torque, and puller** sequence; record **before/after** spectrogram for the reliability library.
