# Line shaft alignment & PM — Conveyance drive stations

## Purpose

Keep **drive shafts**, **couplings**, and **gearmotors** within angular and parallel tolerance so **rotary components** wear predictably and **preventive** work can be planned instead of reacting to **breakdowns**.

## Preventive program elements

- **Quarterly** or runtime-based **alignment verification** (laser or dial per site standard) after **any** bearing or coupling work.
- **Documented** thermal running targets for **bearing housings** at nominal speed.
- **Spares** policy for **couplings**, **seals**, and **matched bearing sets** on critical shafts.

## When alignment issues force breakdown response

- **Rub marks** on coupling guard, **visible coupling gap** change, or **severe** 1× vibration after an impact or jam.
- **Thermal runaway** on **both** motor and driven shaft ends simultaneously.

## Safe work outline

1. LOTO and verify **zero stored energy** in the line (belt/chain slack where required).
2. Measure **runout** and **angular offset**; correct per OEM **shim chart**.
3. Restart with **progressive** speed ramp; capture **post-alignment** baseline for the edge model.
